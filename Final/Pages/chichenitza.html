Chichén Itzá

About the Chichén Itzá

Welcome to Chichén Itzá, a captivating archaeological site located in Mexico's Yucatán Peninsula. As one of the New Seven Wonders of the World, Chichén Itzá stands as a testament to the sophisticated civilization of the Maya people and their remarkable architectural achievements.

The city of Chichén Itzá thrived between the 9th and 12th centuries CE and served as a prominent cultural and political center. The site boasts numerous impressive structures, including the iconic step pyramid known as El Castillo or the Temple of Kukulcan. This pyramid is an architectural marvel, designed with precise alignments and mathematical precision.

Chichén Itzá is also famous for the Great Ball Court, the largest and best-preserved ball court in Mesoamerica. This grand arena witnessed the ancient Maya's sacred ball game, a sport with deep religious and cultural significance.

Other notable structures within Chichén Itzá include the Temple of the Warriors, the Group of a Thousand Columns, and the sacred cenote, a natural sinkhole considered a sacred site for rituals and offerings.

Today, Chichén Itzá attracts visitors from around the world who come to marvel at its architectural splendor and learn about the advanced knowledge and cultural heritage of the ancient Maya civilization.
